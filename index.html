campus life

Auditorium

The very large multi-purpose auditorium is used for hosting various kinds of activity such as assemblies, school concerts and other special events.

Basketball Court

The very large basketball court is used for hosting many basketball competitions as well as basketball practise for the students.

School Shop

The school shop is where the students of OFS (both old and new) can shop for school books/notebooks, clothes, stationery, etc. It also opens some days before the school starts for back-to-school shopping.

Enrichment Program

Clay Making
Eligibility: Kindergarten 2 to Grade 7 students

Clay making encourages out-of-the-box thinking and enables children to explore their creative side. This right brained tactile activity enables children to develop their fine motor skills and 3-D visualisation ability. It is fun and encourages creative movement in children. Through clay modelling, children harness their ability to create. In attending regular pottery making sessions, the child will develop a stronger attention span and be able to focus and concentrate better as well as persevere in completing their project from beginning to end. The pottery curriculum is unique as it breaks the paradigm of traditional pottery making to focus on creativity-development and learning outcomes.

Volleyball
Eligibility: Kindergarten 2 to Grade 12 students

Come Judo with us! Let our passionate instructors share their wealth of experience, to bring out the best in your child. Great emphasis is placed on tumbling and falling techniques to enhance safety for the students on and off the mats. Values of teamwork, resilience and humility are reinforced through controlled sparring. Providing a light to moderate total body workout, with excellent student to trainer ratio, we ensure the highest standards of safety. Certified promotions (recognised worldwide) ensure that your child can continue practising confidently anywhere. Uniform required and certified grading offered upon completion of 24 training sessions. Come journey with us, and explore the many facets of Judo!

Judo
Eligibility: Kindergarten 2 to Grade 6 students

For some, this may be their first experience of a flying ball sport, while for others, this may be a continuation of their previous exposure and interest. Regardless of ability, the Volleyball sessions are designed to be recreational and instructive. The aim is to foster a sense of enjoyment with the sport that will keep students engaged and intrigued to discover more, whilst also introducing and developing the fundamental techniques required to become proficient in the sport of Volleyball. The coaches will use a series of drills to train skills ranging from throwing and catching, to handset, passing, spiking, and service. They will then use games to reinforce the knowledge the students have been gaining, and to spark the competitive appetites that will drive them to new heights

Upcoming Events

  • Grade 4 Model United Nations

    The Grade 4 students are looking forward to their first MUN experience and General Assembly. They will be delegates representing countries from around the world focusing on the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.

  • High School Euclid Math Competition

    The Euclid is a mathematics contest for senior-level high school students that is written by approximately 19,000 students world-wide each year. The contest is produced by the University of Waterloo which is located in Ontario, Canada. Certificates of Distinction will be awarded to students who score in the top 25% of all contestants. Scoring well on the Euclid Mathematics Contest can help students gain admission to the Faculty of Mathematics programs at the University of Waterloo and allow them to be considered for the Faculty of Mathematics Entrance Scholarships.
